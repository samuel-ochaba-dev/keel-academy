# The Contract: Committing to a Tradition

A masterpiece is not defined by hitting one aesthetic target. It is defined by **choosing a tradition and executing its contract without flinching.** Before writing a sentence, commit — and the failure mode to kill is defaulting to tasteful, resolution-resisting, theme-withholding, morally-balanced, opaque-character fiction *regardless of the brief*. That reflex (see "Default-to-ambiguity mush" in `anti-patterns.md`) is genre averaging at the level of aesthetic.

## The commitment procedure

**Commit to a tradition (or a named hybrid) from the menu below. The dials and menus are that tradition's diagnostic readout — its fingerprint — not a separate decision.** Name the commitment; read off its dials and menus; check they cohere. Then apply each of the five pillars *as that contract's law* — keeping the invariants, flipping the contract-specific rules.

## The dials (continuous settings) and menus (pick one, or a named blend)

| Dial | Pole A | Pole B |
|---|---|---|
| Moral stance | Ambiguity (no side declared) | Moral clarity (a vision asserted) |
| Interiority-density | thin interior access | consciousness fully rendered |
| Plot-density | little external event | dense event and consequence |
| Reality model | Realism (mimetic) | Fabulism (myth, fable, the fantastic) |
| Ending | Irresolution (transformed question) | Resolution (reversal + recognition, closure) |
| Prose surface | Transparent (plain, gets out of the way) | Sound-enacting (maximalist, syntax performs meaning) |

**Interiority and plot are independent dials, not a see-saw.** *Crime and Punishment* and *Middlemarch* are maximal on both — dense consciousness *and* dense event. The literary thriller and the capacious novel live in the high-high corner.

| Menu | Options (pick one dominant, optionally one secondary) |
|---|---|
| Register | tragic · comic · ironic · satiric · elegiac · absurd · uncanny |
| Shape | arc · wave · wavelet · meander · spiral · radial/explosion · network/cell · fractal (Jane Alison's repertoire, plus the dramatic arc) |

Shape is a choice from a library, not a dial with two ends. Cross-reference the shape repertoire in `structural-architecture.md`.

## The contract menu (known-coherent clusters)

- **Ambiguous-literary** (James, Flaubert, Chekhov, Woolf) — ambiguity; interiority high, plot low–mid; irresolution; opacity; emergent theme; register elegiac/ironic; shape meander/wave. *The skill's native contract.*
- **Aristotelian-tragic** (Sophocles, Hardy, McCarthy's *The Road*) — plot high; resolution via reversal + recognition; moral weight can be clear; register tragic; shape arc. "Resist resolution" is *wrong* here.
- **Comic** (Austen, Dickens, Fielding) — plot high; resolution/closure; masterful-flat characters welcome; moral clarity common; register comic/satiric; shape arc or network. Closure is the contract.
- **Minimalist / plain-style / iceberg** (Hemingway, Carver, Camus, Coetzee) — restraint; prose transparent, *not* sound-enacting; register often elegiac/ironic. "Sound enacts meaning" is inapplicable.
- **Maximalist / modernist** (McCarthy, DeLillo, Nabokov, Pynchon) — sound enacts meaning; dense syntax. This is where the prose doctrine is literally true.
- **Fabulist / mythic / parable** (García Márquez, Borges, Kafka, Le Guin, Calvino; the fable line: Aesop, late Tolstoy, Orwell) — fabulism; often moral clarity and *stated* theme; masterful-flat characters legitimate; register uncanny/absurd/parabolic; shape radial/fractal.
- **Capacious / "baggy monster"** (Tolstoy, Eliot, Hugo, Grossman) — interiority *and* plot both high; breadth over severe constraint; narrator may generalize morally; shape network. (Henry James's own defended looseness — "large loose baggy monsters," Preface to *The Tragic Muse*, 1908.)

**Authorized hybrids.** Combine two traditions, provided you (a) name the hybrid and (b) keep its readout deliberate. Known-good: **literary thriller** (ambiguous-literary interiority + genre resolution — Le Carré, Highsmith, Tana French); **tragicomedy** (tragic + comic register); **mythic-realist** (realism + fabulism — Morrison, Rushdie). What produces incoherence is not hybridity but *unwitting averaging* — stapling "resist resolution" onto a comic romance because you forgot to check the readout.

## Reading the contract off a brief (signals)

- **"A woman decides whether to leave her marriage over one summer."** Small external stakes, single consciousness under pressure → **ambiguous-literary**: interiority high, plot low, irresolution, register elegiac/ironic, shape meander. (The native contract.)
- **"A con man remakes himself and is destroyed by the past he outran."** Withheld backstory, causal machine, downfall → **Aristotelian-tragic**: plot high, resolution via recognition, register tragic, shape arc. "Resist resolution" is wrong here.
- **"A village where the dead return and no one remarks on it."** → **fabulist**; realism's rules are off. Models: García Márquez, Kafka, Le Guin — not Eliot.
- **"A grifter's schemes collide until everyone gets their comeuppance."** → **comic**: plot high, closure, masterful-flat cast, register comic/satiric. Austen/Dickens closure is the target; "resist resolution" breaks the promise.
- **"A parable about the cost of obedience."** → **fabulist / moral-clarity**; theme *should* be legible (Orwell, late Tolstoy). "Never state theme" is a genre violation here.
- **"Three generations, four cities, a century."** → **capacious**: interiority *and* plot both high; narrator may moralize; shape network. "Severe formal constraint" and "opacity" are optional; breadth and legibility are load-bearing.
- **"A defector's handler realizes the mole is his own protégé."** → **hybrid literary thriller**: interiority high *and* plot high *and* genre resolution. The coherence rule must *permit* this, not flag it.
- **Genre signals** (mystery, romance, quest): resolution is the *contract*, not a weakness.

## The invariants (kept as law in every contract)

Only these hold across all traditions; everything else is a choice:

1. **A governing sensibility, never a generic default** — the language commits to a coherent sensibility, whether idiosyncratic (modernist fingerprint) or deliberately traditional/communal (parable, myth, King-James cadence). (Retains the anti-"literary voice" test.)
2. **Deliberate narratorial stance and distance** — where the narration stands is chosen, not accidental; it may hold one distance (essayistic/mythic) or modulate (novelistic realism).
3. **Desire + coherence** — characters (or governing forces) want something and cohere; desire drives causality. Holds for flat and round alike.
4. **Specificity / "thisness"** — concrete particularity over abstraction. "A mother returning the dead son's shoes to the closet," not "grief."
5. **Necessity / integration** — every element earns its place; structure is argument; removing a load-bearing part damages the whole.
6. **Earned effect** — no emotion, reversal, or meaning arrives without prepared cause. (Tragic catharsis, comic delight, ambiguous unease equally.)
7. **Meaning exceeds event** — the work means more than its literal happenings, whether that meaning is stated or emergent.
