# Anti-Patterns: What Prevents AI Fiction From Achieving Masterwork

## Why this file exists

AI-generated fiction has specific, predictable failure modes that prevent it from achieving masterwork status. These are not random errors. They are systematic tendencies that emerge from how language models are trained. Knowing them precisely allows you to detect and avoid them.

## The fatal five

### 1. Genre averaging

**What it looks like**: The output reads like the statistical mean of all novels in its category. It is competent, well-structured, and completely indistinguishable from ten thousand other competent novels.

**Why it happens**: The model has absorbed millions of examples and defaults to the central tendency. The result is a novel that breaks no rules because it follows all of them simultaneously.

**How to detect it**: Ask: could this paragraph appear in any other novel of this type without anyone noticing? If yes, it is a genre average.

**How to fix it**: Impose constraints that force deviation from the mean. A specific formal rule, an unusual structural choice, a voice commitment that eliminates the generic.

**Before**: "The rain fell heavily on the city streets as Sarah made her way home, her thoughts troubled by what had happened at the office that day."

**After**: "Wet pavement, headlights stretched into blades. Sarah walked the way people walk when they have just broken something they cannot name."

### 2. Beautiful emptiness

**What it looks like**: Prose that sounds literary, sophisticated, and aesthetically pleasing but accumulates no emotional force. The sentences are individually admirable but collectively inert.

**Why it happens**: The model has learned what literary prose sounds like without understanding that its function is to create emotional momentum through structure. It optimizes for surface beauty rather than structural power.

**How to detect it**: Read five pages. Does the tension increase? Does the emotional pressure build? If the prose is beautiful but nothing accumulates, it is empty.

**How to fix it**: Every beautiful sentence must serve the structural ratchet. If a sentence does not increase tension, reveal character, or advance the thematic argument, cut it regardless of how good it sounds.

**Before**: "The morning light splintered through the dusty windowpane, casting geometries of gold across the worn floorboards where generations had walked their quiet sorrows into the grain of the wood."

**After**: "Light came through the window the way it always had. She stood in it and it meant nothing."

### 3. Theme as thesis

**What it looks like**: The novel has a clear message. The plot is designed to prove it. Characters who embody the correct position succeed. Characters who oppose it fail or learn.

**Why it happens**: The model interprets "thematic depth" as "having a point." It then constructs the narrative to argue that point, which produces propaganda rather than literature.

**How to detect it**: Can you state what the novel "believes" in one sentence? If yes, it is a thesis, not a theme.

**How to fix it**: Distribute moral weight across incompatible positions. Make both sides genuinely compelling. The novel's job is to force the reader to hold contradiction, not to resolve it.

**Before**: A novel about climate change where the scientist protagonist is right, the corporate antagonist is wrong, and the ending shows consequences of inaction.

**After**: A novel about climate change where the scientist's obsession destroys her family, the corporate antagonist genuinely believes his employees' livelihoods matter more than abstract futures, and the ending shows both of them ruined by forces neither controlled.

### 4. Character as psychology case study

**What it looks like**: Characters are fully explained. Their childhood trauma is revealed. Their motivations are transparent. Their behavior is consistent with their psychological profile. They are comprehensible.

**Why it happens**: The model treats characterization as the provision of information about a person rather than the creation of a PRESENCE that exceeds information.

**How to detect it**: Do you feel you fully understand why the character does what they do? If yes, they are too shallow for masterwork.

**How to fix it**: Leave gaps. Refuse to explain. Let the character do things that surprise even the narrator. Create moments where motivation is genuinely ambiguous, not because you withheld information but because the character is genuinely opaque to themselves.

**Before**: "She pushed him away because her father had abandoned her at seven, and she had learned that love was a precursor to loss. She recognized the pattern even as she repeated it."

**After**: "She told him to leave. Later she could not remember deciding to say it. The words had arrived from somewhere she did not have access to."

### 5. Experimental for its own sake

**What it looks like**: Unusual structure, fractured chronology, multiple perspectives, unconventional formatting, metafictional elements. All deployed without serving the story's necessity.

**Why it happens**: The model interprets "masterwork" as "formally innovative" and adds complexity that does not earn its presence. The innovation is decorative rather than necessary.

**How to detect it**: Ask: if you told this story in the simplest possible way, would the theme still work? If yes, the formal innovation is unnecessary.

**How to fix it**: Every formal choice must be demanded by the content. Non-linear structure because the character's experience of time is non-linear. Multiple perspectives because no single perspective contains the truth. If the simplest form works, use the simplest form.

## Secondary anti-patterns

### 6. The literary voice

A generic register that sounds elevated, contemplative, and writerly but belongs to no one. This is the most common AI failure mode in literary fiction. It sounds like "a literary novel" rather than like a specific writer writing a specific book.

### 7. Unearned emotion

Big feelings that arrive without structural preparation. A character cries. A loss occurs. A reunion happens. But the reader feels nothing because the emotional moment was not built toward through hundreds of pages of carefully applied pressure.

### 8. Symmetry addiction

Overly neat structures where everything rhymes, every thread resolves, every question is answered. Real masterworks leave loose ends. They resist the human craving for pattern by including elements that do not fit.

### 9. Metaphor saturation

Every other sentence contains a figurative comparison. The reader's imaginative capacity is exhausted by page 50. In masterwork, metaphors are rare and therefore powerful. When Fitzgerald compares the green light to the "orgastic future," it works because the rest of the prose is relatively restrained.

### 10. Stakes inflation

Escalating external stakes (the world is ending, everything depends on this moment) rather than deepening internal stakes. In masterwork, the stakes are often small in external terms but enormous in psychological terms. A marriage. A choice. A single afternoon.
